## Overview

As AI models become integral to safety-critical maintenance decisions, **explainability** is essential. This project develops interpretable AI methods for multimodal predictive maintenance of jet engines, bridging the gap between model accuracy and human understanding.

## Key Research Thrusts

- **Explainable Multi-Modal Sensing** -- Developing methods that provide transparent reasoning over heterogeneous sensor data (vibration, thermal, acoustic) for engine health monitoring.
- **Explainable AI for Machine Health Monitoring** -- Applying state-of-the-art XAI techniques (attention visualization, feature attribution, concept-based explanations) to fault diagnosis and RUL prediction models.
- **Physics-Informed Deep Learning** -- Integrating domain knowledge and physical constraints into neural network architectures to produce predictions that are both accurate and physically consistent.

## Research Goals

- Enable maintenance engineers to **trust and verify** AI-driven recommendations
- Provide **actionable insights** into which sensor channels and temporal patterns drive predictions
- Develop **hybrid models** that combine data-driven learning with physics-based priors
