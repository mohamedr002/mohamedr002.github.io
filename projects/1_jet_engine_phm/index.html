AI-Driven Predictive Maintenance for Jet Engines

AI solutions for prognostics and health management of jet engines

Overview

This project develops advanced AI solutions for prognostics and health management (PHM) of jet engines, addressing critical challenges in anomaly detection, fault diagnostics, and remaining useful life (RUL) estimation.

Key Research Thrusts

  • Anomaly Detection & Fault Diagnostics – Building deep learning models that detect early signs of engine degradation and accurately diagnose fault types from multimodal sensor data.
  • Remaining Useful Life Estimation – Predicting when engine components will fail, enabling proactive maintenance scheduling and reducing unplanned downtime.
  • Multimodal Integration with Large Language Models – Leveraging LLMs for comprehensive engine health monitoring by fusing heterogeneous data sources including vibration, thermal, and operational parameters.

Funded Projects

  • Co-PI, AI-Based Predictive Maintenance for Jet Engines, USD 300K, Technology Innovation Institute (TII), 2025–2026
  • PI, AI-Based IR Radiation Prediction for Engines, 2025–2026

Related Research Areas

Our work in this area builds on foundations in domain adaptation, RUL prediction, and anomaly detection, connecting to our broader research on transfer learning for time series.
